Gravity

By now you know that an object will not move from its state of rest unless an external force acts on it.

So if you were to simply drop an object from a certain height, why does it fall towards the ground? What force is acting on the object?

In 8th grade when you studied about different kinds of forces, you learnt about gravitational force.

This force is the reason why all objects dropped from a certain height always fall towards the ground.

Gravitational force is also the reason why the moon revolves around the earth and the earth revolves around the sun!


The attractional force of gravitation is not one way. All objects in the universe attract other objects with a force that is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the distance between them.

Free fall

We noted right at the beginning of the chapter that objects dropped from a height fall towards the ground due to the earth's gravitational force.

But do you think all objects fall towards earth at the same rate?

To answer this question, we first have to learn about the phenomenon of free fall.

Whenever an object falls towards earth solely under the influence of gravity, the object is said to be in free fall.

While in free fall, there is a change in the velocity of the object and any change in velocity involves a change in acceleration.

The accleration due to the gravitational force of earth is denoted by g.

The force of attraction of the earth on an object is known as the weight(W) of an object.
W = m*g

The weight of an object can differ on different planets, as each planet has its own 'g', however the mass remains the same.
