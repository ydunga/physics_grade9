Law of conservation of momentum

An extension of the third law of motion, is the law of conservation of momentum.

According to this law if two objects collide with each other , in an ideal system (no external force acting on the objects), then the momenta (plural of momentum) of both objects before and after the collision is the same i.e the momentum is conserved.

How is this law related to the third law of motion?

Click on the first red ball shown below.

In this illustration we have two balls - ball A and ball B, moving in the same direction but with different velocities.

When the two objects collide, each will exert an equal amount of force on each other, but in opposite directions.

Let FAB be the force that ball A exerts on ball B and let F BA be the force that ball B exerts on ball A.

Then according to the third law of motion -

FAB = - FBA

We had established that Force = mass * acceleration. So let's put in the mass and acceleration of each ball into the above equation.


If you then equate and FAB to -FBA, then you will realise the sum of the momentum of the two balls, before and after the collision is the same.
