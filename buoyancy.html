Buoyancy

Have you ever noticed that when you drop an object, like a plastic bottle in a container full of water, the object floats on the water.

Why is it that the object in water does not fall to the ground? Is the downward pull of the earth's gravitation being countered by another force?

Buoyancy

Water or any liquid for that matter, exerts an upward force on the plastic bottle. The force exerted by the water on the bottle is greater than its weight (W = m*g) and hence the plastic bottle floats on water.

This upward thrust exerted by water is known as buoyancy.

Whether an object sinks or floats, when dropped in water depends on its density.

The density of a substance is defined as the mass per unit of volume.

If an object sinks in water, this means it has greater density than water. Whereas if it floats, that means the object's density is lower than that of water.

We can define the density of an object relative to water. This is known as relative density.
