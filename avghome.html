Electricity grids

In order to supply electrical energy to millions of homes in a country, a grid of electricity distribution is needed.

The process of electricity generation usually begins in a power station.

Most power stations in the world burn fossil fuels like coal and natural gas to power their generators which convert mechanical power to electric power.

The power plant is usually connected to the electric grid.

The grid itself consists of the power plant and the further transmission channels.

The interconnected lines which transport electricity from the plant to the end customer are known as the transmission network.

You must have seen the tall electric poles with many long wires connected to them. These poles are part of the transmission network.

From the transmission network the electricity reaches your homes and streetlights and allow you to do things like switching on the light below.

Click on the button to turn on the light!


On average and Indian household consumes 90 kWh of electricity per month. In countries like the USA, the average energy consumption of households is much higher.
