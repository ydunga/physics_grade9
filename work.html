Work

The reason we care about concepts like force, pressure, thrust and buoyancy is because we use them to perform work.


In scientific terms, work is the energy transferred from one form to another, along a displacement.

Any object possessing energy is capable of doing work, by exerting force on another object.The unit of measurement of work and energy is J - joule.

There are different kinds of energy, which we will explore below.

Kinetic energy

A moving object, possess energy and hence can do work. The energy that an object possesses due to its motion is known as kinetic energy.

Above, you can see a wind turbine. The turbine converts the kinetic energy of air into electric energy, which we can use to power appliances.

Potential energy

Just like we can harness the kinetic energy of wind through windmills, we can harness the energy of water using turbines.

When flowing water falls on on a turbine, it can make the turbine generate electrical energy. However, in order to generate a large amount of energy we need either a large amount of water of water moving at very high speed.

In modern day dams, water is stored in large reservoirs. This water then flows down at rapid speed onto turbines, which generate hydroelectricity.

Energy that gets stored due to work done on an object(solid or fluid) is known as potential energy.

Gravitational potential energy

When you raise an object (solid or liquid) to a certain height, the object acquire energy.

This is because work is done on the object against gravity while it is being raised.

This energy stored in the object due to its height is known as gravitational potential energy.

This is why in dams, water is dropped from a height. The tallest dam on earth is the Nurek dam is Tajikistan, which is 300 meters tall!

Now that you know what potential energy is, how will you determine how much work is done by an object with a certain mass(m), falling from a certain height(h).

W (work done) = force X displacement
force = mg
displacement = h
which means W = mgh

You could also say that the potential energy of the object is mgh Joules.
