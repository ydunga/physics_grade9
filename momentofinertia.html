Why care about inertia

We've just read about what inertia is. Inertia is the property of an object to stay in its state of rest.

A related concept is that of moment of inertia or rotational inertia.

Rotational inertia of an object tells you how difficult it is to change the rotational velocity (how fast an object rotates) of an object around a rotational axis.

For example, you know that the earth rotates about its axis.

The rotational inertia of an object not only depends on its mass, but it also depends on the distribution of the mass relative to its axis of rotation.

A great example of rotational inertia in use is the spinning of ice skaters.

Ice skaters can significantly increase their speed of spinning/rotation, by pulling their hands towards their body.

When the mass of an object is away from its axis of rotation it becomes increasingly difficult to change the rotational velocity of the object.

If however the mass of the object shifts towards the axis of rotation, the moment of inertia of the object decreases.

The angle at which the rotation is taking place is called angular momentum and is usually conserved, i.e it doesn't change unless an external force acts on it.

The angular momentum remains the same, while the moment of inertia decreases, which means the angular velocity (speed of rotation) increases!
