Motion

Here you can see the red ball in two kinds of motion. In the first instance, the ball is moving on a plain surface along a straight line. Whereas in the second instance the ball is moving in a circular motion.

In simple terms, motion is the movement of an object from one point to another.



The magnitude of motion measures the physical distance an object may have covered in its motion. The distance can be measured in centimeter, meter, kilometer or any of the standard units of measurement for length/distance.

Do you know what the distance between Delhi and Mumbai is?

The road distance between the two cities in 1408km.

The displacement of an object, on the other hand, tells us how far the object is from its starting position after motion.

Different objects take varying amounts of time to cover a given distance.

In the illustration above, both the circles are covering the same distance. The red circle takes 10 seconds to finish the route, while the pink cirlce takes 5 seconds.

We can measure how much distance is being covered by each circle per unit of time.

In our case we would be measuring, how many pixels are the circles moving forward by in each second.

This measure is known as speed. The unit for measurement of speed is m s -1.

Do you remember the distance between Delhi and Mumbai? It was 1408km.

If you were to drive every day, for 10 hours at the speed of 50km per hour, it would take you 2.8 days to cover the distance.

However, if you were to take a plane, it would take you only 2 hours.

The speed of an object need not be constant over the entire distance.

You can see in the illustration below that the speed of the circle changes with time.

This is why, when we describe the rate of motion of an object, we talk about average speed.


Average speed can be obtained by dividing the total distance travelled by total time taken.


We can also represent distance travelled in a certain time period using graphs.

Velocity

Motion is not always along a straight path. realistically, objects change direction when in motion.

When we add the directional quality to speed we can better describe the motion of an object. This measure is known as velocity.

The main difference when calculating speed vs velocity is that instead of distance we are now measuring displacement.

Average velocity can be calculated the same way as average speed.
